# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Gamers who purchased access from you. Desktop-focused users who expect premium quality and exclusivity. They're used to polished gaming experiences (Steam, Discord, Epic Games) and expect the same level of craft in other software they use.

Context: They access this during evening hours, post-work or weekend entertainment sessions. They're in relaxation mode, not productivity mode. They expect smooth performance and thoughtful design that respects their time.

## Product Purpose

A private anime streaming platform for your customers. This is not a public tool or open-source project. It's an exclusive service that matches the quality of the games they purchase from you.

Success means users feel they're accessing something premium and well-crafted. They should recognize immediately that this is different from generic anime sites, built with the same attention to detail they expect from premium gaming software.

## Brand Personality

Premium, exclusive, polished

The interface should feel like it belongs alongside high-quality desktop gaming software. Not flashy or gimmicky, but confident in its refinement. Restrained rather than loud. Quality signals through craft, not decoration.

## Anti-references

- **Generic purple anime sites** (MyAnimeList, Anilist clones with the same purple + dark + glassmorphism formula)
- **Overused glassmorphism** with purple gradients and blur effects used decoratively
- **Cluttered free streaming sites** with ads, popups, and low-quality UI
- **Open-source tool aesthetic** that looks utilitarian and bland, signaling "community project" rather than "premium service"
- **Gaming RGB clichés** (rainbow colors, aggressive neon, "gamer aesthetic" tropes)

## Design Principles

### 1. Exclusive by design
Visual language should signal "this is premium, not public." Choices should feel intentional and refined, never accidental or default. Users should recognize quality through restraint and craft.

### 2. Gaming-grade polish
Match the quality expectations of your gaming customers. They know what good software feels like. Smooth animations, responsive interactions, attention to micro-details. No rough edges.

### 3. Desktop-optimized
Maximize screen real estate for desktop users. Don't compromise desktop experience for mobile-first patterns. This is a desktop application that happens to run in a browser.

### 4. Confident minimalism
Show restraint, not feature bloat. Every element earns its place. Information density without clutter. Trust that less can be more when executed with precision.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- **WCAG 2.1 AA** compliance minimum for color contrast and interaction patterns
- **Keyboard navigation** required throughout (gamers expect full keyboard control)
- **Reduced motion support** critical for photosensitive users (respect `prefers-reduced-motion`)
- **Focus indicators** must be clear and visible for keyboard navigation
- **Screen reader support** for core flows (login, browsing, playback controls)

Accessibility is not an afterthought. It's part of the premium quality promise.
